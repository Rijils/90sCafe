Why should a 90s kid consider themselves lucky?

“ Today is the first day of the rest of your life.”

We all know that school life was the golden period of our lives but gone are the days for every 90s kid. We often say we don't realize the value of the moment till it's gone, the same goes with school memories. Also, 90s school kids were probably the last generation that spent time outside playing with friends and not on mobile games. 90s kids have school friends, not Facebook friends. We were the last generation of kids who truly knew how to be kids. These days, playing outside has been replaced by sitting inside texting, playing video games, and surfing the internet. All of us have great memories from the 90s school life as opposed to social media life and digital networking. So, let's relive some beautiful memories of school life during the 90s.

Why 90s kids are awesome !!

" If You Carry Your Childhood With You, You Never Old ! "

We always wait for Shaktimaan, on weekends, and Summer vacation used to be spent at grannies' houses, eating mangoes, etc. And completing vacation homework on the last days. We used to watch shakala ka boom boom and also carried that pencil. On birthday, your best friend used to get the opportunity to miss classes so that you can go for distributing chocolates to other teachers. If the class monitor is your friend, then you have Liberty to talk in a free hour ( they didn't use to write your name). Nirma : Hema, Rekha, Jaya aur shushma ,sabko pasand Nirma (everyone's choice is Nirma).

Beautiful Moments...

Those born in the 90s have witnessed a great childhood as well as childhoods being robbed by technology now. Hence we know the difference. We know what is supposed to matter to us.

Festivals

Vishu, Christmas, Bakrid, Easter, and Ramadan, all these festivals are celebrated in Kerala. Onam is considered the state festival.

Weather

Kerala follows different climatic features since it is encapsulated between the mighty Western Ghats and the Arabian Sea.

Technology

Television channel operated by India's national broadcaster Doordarshan, We watched Doordarshan on TV.

Life Style

We were close to humans and nature, not technology. The morning walk was the weekend ritual. Fresh air, sunlight, and my best friend gave me company.
